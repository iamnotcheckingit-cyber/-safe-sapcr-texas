M

Marcus T.

Harris County, Texas
Victory

I was deployed overseas when my ex filed for sole custody. She knew my APO address—we'd been communicating regularly about our daughter. But her attorney filed a Certificate of Last Known Address listing a house I hadn't lived in for three years.

By the time I returned stateside, a default judgment had stripped me of all custody rights. I was devastated. My daughter was told I had "abandoned" her.

"The court system that's supposed to protect families was weaponized against mine. But I didn't give up."

With help from a veterans' legal aid organization, I filed a Bill of Review. We obtained email records proving opposing counsel knew my current address. After 14 months of fighting, the default judgment was vacated.

Outcome

Default judgment vacated. Joint managing conservatorship restored. Reunified with daughter after 2 years.
S

Sarah K.

Dallas County, Texas
Victory

My ex-husband's attorney served papers to my parents' address—a house I moved out of when I was 18. I was 34 when this happened. They knew where I lived; I was still in the same apartment complex where we'd exchanged our kids for visitation.

The default judgment modified our custody agreement, giving him primary custody and reducing my time with my children to four days a month.

"I found out about the custody change when I went to pick up my kids and they weren't there."

I represented myself pro se initially because I couldn't afford an attorney. After connecting with SAFE SAPCR and learning about Bill of Review procedures, I filed one myself. The court found extrinsic fraud and set aside the judgment.

Outcome

Bill of Review granted. Original custody order reinstated. Now advocating for other pro se parents.
J

James R.

Tarrant County, Texas
Fighting

My case is still pending, but I want other parents to know they're not alone. I discovered a default judgment had been entered against me when I tried to enroll my son in little league and learned I was no longer listed as a legal guardian.

The citation was allegedly posted on the door of a vacant lot. There's no house there—just an empty field. But someone signed an affidavit saying service was complete.

"Every day I fight is a day closer to seeing my son again. I won't stop."

My Bill of Review hearing is scheduled for next month. I've gathered affidavits from neighbors, photos of the vacant lot, and records showing my actual address was on file with multiple state agencies.

Status

Bill of Review pending. Hearing scheduled. Supported by SAFE SAPCR community.
R

Rosa M.

Bexar County, Texas
Victory

English is my second language, and I believe my ex's attorney counted on that. The documents were complex, the process confusing. When papers were left at my old workplace—a job I'd left six months earlier—I never saw them.

The default judgment terminated my parental rights entirely. My children were told their mother didn't want them anymore. That's a lie that still haunts me.

"They tried to erase me from my children's lives. But a mother's love cannot be erased by paperwork."

A legal aid attorney took my case pro bono after hearing my story at a community event. It took two years, but we proved the service was fraudulent. I was reunited with my children on their birthdays—the best gift I ever received.

Outcome

Parental rights fully restored. Now volunteers as Spanish-language interpreter for other families in court.
D

David L.

Travis County, Texas
Fighting

I'm a software engineer. I work from home. I'm always here. But according to court records, I was served in person at an address 200 miles away on a day I have GPS data proving I was in Austin.

Someone forged my signature on the return of service. I've never seen that handwriting in my life.

"When you have evidence of fraud this clear and the system still makes you fight for years, something is broken."

My case has been covered by local news. I've testified before the legislature. I won't just win my case—I'm going to help change the law so this can't happen to other parents.

Status

Investigating forged service documents. Working with legislators on reform. Active SAFE SAPCR advocate.
