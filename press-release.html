The Mission

SAFE SAPCR Texas advocates for reform of Texas Rule 239a, which currently allows attorneys to certify a respondent's "last known address" without any verification requirement. This loophole enables service of process to wrong addresses, resulting in default judgments against parents who never received notice of custody proceedings.

The SAFE SAPCR Act would require:

  • Mandatory constable service for all SAPCR cases
  • Attorney due diligence certification with documentation
  • Criminal penalties for fraudulent certificates
  • Expedited remedies for fraud victims
