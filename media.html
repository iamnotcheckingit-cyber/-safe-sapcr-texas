Key Talking Points

Understanding the Issue

  • The Loophole: Texas Rule 239a allows attorneys to certify a respondent's "last known address" without any verification requirement. This enables service of process to wrong addresses, resulting in default judgments against people who never received notice.
  • Due Process Violation: When parents don't receive notice of custody proceedings, they lose their constitutional right to be heard. Children can be separated from loving parents through paperwork, not evidence.
  • No Remedy: The only current remedy is a Bill of Review—an expensive, time-consuming legal proceeding that most affected parents cannot afford.
  • The Solution: The SAFE SAPCR Act would require mandatory constable service, attorney due diligence certification, and expedited remedies for fraud victims.
  • Bipartisan Issue: This is not a partisan issue. Parents of all backgrounds are affected. The solution protects constitutional rights that everyone agrees are fundamental.
