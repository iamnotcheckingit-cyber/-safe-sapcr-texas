Texas SAPCR: Get Started Guide

Your Quick-Reference Checklist for Custody Cases & Parental Rights

SAFE SAPCR Texas | safesapcrtx.org

What is SAPCR?

SAPCR stands for Suit Affecting the Parent-Child Relationship. It's the legal proceeding in Texas used to establish or modify:

Conservatorship (Custody)

  • Managing conservator (decision-making)
  • Possessory conservator (visitation)
  • Joint managing conservatorship

Other Issues

  • Child support amounts
  • Possession schedules
  • Medical support/insurance
  • Paternity establishment

Are You Affected? Checklist

Check any that apply to your situation:

  • You never received custody papers but learned a case was filed against you
  • Papers were served to the wrong address — not where you actually lived
  • A default judgment was entered without your knowledge or participation
  • You discovered a custody order exists that you didn't know about
  • Someone filed a false address with the court as your "last known address"
  • Service was by posting or publication when you could have been found
If you checked any box above: You may be a victim of service fraud or improper service of process. This is grounds for a Bill of Review to set aside the default judgment. Time limits may apply — consult an attorney immediately.

Immediate Steps If You've Been Affected

Step 1: Gather Documentation

  • Proof of your actual address at the time of alleged service (lease, utility bills, mail, W-2)
  • Court records showing the Certificate of Last Known Address filed
  • Service documents (citation, return of service, publication notices)
  • Timeline of when you learned about the case and what steps you've taken

Step 2: Understand Your Legal Options

Bill of Review

  • Sets aside void/voidable judgments
  • Requires proving fraud, accident, or wrongful act
  • Filed as separate lawsuit in same court
  • No strict deadline (but act quickly)

Motion for New Trial

  • Must file within 30 days of judgment
  • Used for recent judgments only
  • Lower burden of proof
  • Same case, not new lawsuit

Step 3: Take Action

  • Consult a family law attorney experienced in Bill of Review cases
  • File a complaint with the State Bar if attorney misconduct occurred
  • Document everything — keep copies of all communications and filings
  • Contact your legislator — reform requires constituent pressure

Key Terms to Know

Certificate of Last Known Address (Rule 239a): A sworn statement an attorney must file before using service by posting/publication. Currently requires NO verification.

Extrinsic Fraud: Fraud that prevents you from having a fair opportunity to present your case — like filing a false address or hiding service attempts.

Default Judgment: A court ruling entered when one party fails to respond. If you weren't properly served, this judgment may be voidable.

The Solution: SAFE SAPCR Act

We're advocating for Texas legislation that would:

  • Require constable service in all SAPCR cases (no more cheap alternatives)
  • Mandate due diligence before certifying addresses
  • Create expedited Bill of Review hearings (45 days, not years)
  • Add criminal penalties for fraudulent address certification
Your voice matters. Contact your state representative and senator. Tell them: "Support the SAFE SAPCR Act to protect Texas families from custody fraud."
