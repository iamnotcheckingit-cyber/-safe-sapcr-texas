The SAFE SAPCR Act

Service Accountability and Fraud Elimination in Suits Affecting the Parent-Child Relationship

Download the Draft Legislation

Review the complete text of the proposed SAFE SAPCR Act, designed to protect Texas families from extrinsic fraud and abuse of process in custody cases.

Download PDF

Key Provisions

  • Mandatory Address Verification

    Requires petitioners to provide documented evidence of the respondent's current address, not just a "last known address" without verification.

  • Enhanced Service Requirements

    Mandates multiple service attempts at different times of day and requires photographic or GPS documentation of service attempts.

  • Citation by Publication Safeguards

    Requires petitioners to exhaust all reasonable means of locating the respondent before resorting to citation by publication, including skip tracing and social media searches.

  • Mandatory Waiting Period

    Extends the waiting period before a default judgment can be entered in SAPCR cases to ensure respondents have adequate time to respond.

  • Attorney Accountability

    Holds attorneys personally liable for knowingly filing false certificates of last known address or facilitating fraudulent service.

  • Simplified Bill of Review Process

    Streamlines the process for victims of fraudulent default judgments to challenge and overturn these orders.

  • Criminal Penalties

    Establishes criminal penalties for intentional fraud in the service of process in family law cases.
